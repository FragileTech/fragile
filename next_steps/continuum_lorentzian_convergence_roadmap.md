## Executive Summary

This document identifies the **minimal set of theorems** required to prove that the discrete Lorentzian structure on the Fractal Set (CST+IG) converges to a smooth Lorentzian manifold in the continuum limit. It also addresses the **information preservation problem**: whether the discrete graph structure retains all information from the N-particle Fragile Gas dynamics, enabling reuse of existing mean-field proofs.

**Key Finding**: The classical Wick rotation approach is **blocked** by nonlinearity and non-analyticity (see Gemini analysis 2025-10-10). The viable path is **direct geometric convergence** of the discrete Lorentzian structure.

**Two Main Questions**:
1. **Continuum Limit**: Does the discrete graph ‚Üí smooth Lorentzian manifold?
2. **Information Preservation**: Can we recover N-particle dynamics from the graph?

## Part I: Continuum Limit Convergence Program

### 1.1. The Target Theorem

:::{prf:theorem} Continuum Lorentzian Limit (TARGET)
:label: thm-continuum-lorentzian-target

Let $\{G_a\}_{a>0}$ be a sequence of Fractal Set graphs generated by the Adaptive Gas with characteristic scale $a$ (episode duration $\tau_e \sim a$, spatial separation $d_g(e,e') \sim a$).

Assume:
1. Episode density: $\mathcal{N}(V_4) \sim V_4/a^4$ (4D volume scaling)
2. IG connectivity: Each episode has $\sim (R/a)^3$ neighbors within distance $R$
3. CST structure: Consistent time-ordering with average branching factor $b \sim O(1)$

Then as $a \to 0$:

**A. Metric Convergence**: There exists a smooth Lorentzian manifold $(M, g_{\mu\nu})$ with signature $(-,+,+,+)$ such that:

$$
g_{ij}^{(a)}(e) \to g_{\mu\nu}(x) \quad \text{in } C^k \text{ topology}
$$

where $g_{ij}^{(a)}$ is the discrete metric induced by fitness Hessian + graph structure.

**B. Causal Structure Convergence**: The discrete causal order $\prec_{\text{CST}}$ converges to the Lorentzian causal order $\prec_g$.

**C. Field Equation Convergence**: The discrete Klein-Gordon equation:

$$
(-\Box_a + m^2) \phi_a = J_a
$$

converges weakly to the continuum equation:

$$
(-\partial_\mu \partial^\mu + m^2) \phi = J
$$

**D. Geodesic Convergence**: Particle trajectories (CST paths) converge to null/timelike geodesics of $(M, g_{\mu\nu})$.
:::

**Status**: ‚ö†Ô∏è **NOT PROVEN** - This is the ultimate goal.

#### **Theorem 1.2.5: d'Alembertian Convergence**

:::{prf:theorem} Discrete d'Alembertian ‚Üí Continuum d'Alembertian
:label: thm-dalembertian-convergence

The discrete d'Alembertian operator:

$$
\Box_a \phi(e) := -\frac{(\partial_0^+ \phi)(e) - (\partial_0^- \phi)(e)}{\bar{\tau}_e} + \sum_{e' \in IG(e)} w_{ee'} (\phi(e') - \phi(e))
$$

converges to the continuum d'Alembertian:

$$
\Box = g^{\mu\nu} \nabla_\mu \nabla_\nu = -\partial_t^2 + \Delta_g
$$

in the sense that for smooth test fields $\phi \in C^\infty_c(M)$:

$$
\left| \sum_{e \in \mathcal{V}_a} (\Box_a \phi)(e) \mu(e) - \int_M (\Box \phi) \sqrt{|g|} \, d^4x \right| \le C a^2 \|\phi\|_{C^4}
$$
:::

**Proof Status**: ‚ö†Ô∏è **PARTIAL (proven for flat regular lattice, not for Fractal Set)**

**What's Proven**:
- Regular (1+1)D lattice case in [lorentzian_signature_rigorous_formalization.md](../docs/source/13_fractal_set/discussions/lorentzian_signature_rigorous_formalization.md)
- Convergence rate $O(a^2)$ for spatial part (standard finite difference)
- Convergence rate $O(a)$ for temporal part (asymmetric stencil)

**What's Missing**:
- Extension to **irregular graph** (Fractal Set has varying edge lengths)
- Handling of **curved background** (emergent $g_{\mu\nu}$ is not flat)
- Proof for **varying episode density** (not uniform)

**Strategy**:
1. Use discrete de Rham-Hodge theory (Desbrun et al. 2005)
2. Prove consistency: $\Box_a \phi(e) = (\Box \phi)(x_e) + O(a^2)$ for smooth $\phi$
3. Show stability: solution of $\Box_a u_a = f_a$ converges to solution of $\Box u = f$

### 2.2. Episode-to-Trajectory Reconstruction

#### **Theorem 2.2.1: Trajectory Reconstruction from CST**

:::{prf:theorem} Episodes Determine Particle Histories
:label: thm-trajectory-reconstruction

Given a Fractal Set graph $G = (\mathcal{V}, E_C, E_I)$ generated by the Adaptive Gas with N walkers over time interval $[0, T]$:

**Forward Direction** (Encoding):
Each walker trajectory $(x_i(t), v_i(t))_{t \in [0,T]}$ generates a genealogical path through CST:
$$
\text{Path}_i = (e_{i,1}, e_{i,2}, \ldots, e_{i,K_i})
$$
where $e_{i,k}$ is the episode created at cloning event $k$ by walker $i$ or its ancestors.

**Inverse Direction** (Reconstruction):
From the CST structure alone, can we uniquely reconstruct:
1. Walker positions $\{x_i(t_k)\}_{i=1}^N$ at each cloning time $t_k$?
2. Walker velocities $\{v_i(t_k)\}_{i=1}^N$?
3. Fitness values $\{V_{\text{fit}}(x_i, S_{t_k})\}$?
4. Cloning weights $\{w_i(t_k)\}$?
:::

**Analysis**:

**What IS stored in episodes** (from current implementation):
- Episode position $x_e \in \mathcal{X}$
- Episode creation time $\tau_e$ (duration)
- Parent episode(s) $P(e)$ (CST structure)
- IG neighbors $IG(e)$ (spatial connectivity)

**What is NOT directly stored**:
- Velocity $v_e$ at episode creation ‚ùå
- Fitness value $V_{\text{fit}}(x_e)$ ‚ùå
- Cloning weight $w_e$ ‚ùå
- Virtual reward history ‚ùå

**Consequence**: **Current Fractal Set does NOT preserve all information** ‚ö†Ô∏è

---

#### **Theorem 2.2.2: Enriched Episode Structure (Proposed Fix)**

:::{prf:definition} Enriched Episode
:label: def-enriched-episode

An **enriched episode** $e$ stores:
1. Position: $x_e \in \mathcal{X}$
2. Velocity: $v_e \in \mathbb{R}^d_v$
3. Creation time: $\tau_e > 0$
4. Fitness: $F_e = V_{\text{fit}}(x_e, S_{\tau_e})$
5. Cloning weight: $w_e = \exp(\alpha r_e - \beta h_e)$
6. Parent link: $P(e) \subseteq \mathcal{V}$
7. Spatial links: $IG(e) \subseteq \mathcal{V}$
:::

:::{prf:theorem} Full Information Preservation
:label: thm-full-information-preservation

If the Fractal Set uses enriched episodes, then:

**A. Trajectory Reconstruction**:
For each walker $i$ at time $t$, the full phase space state $(x_i(t), v_i(t))$ can be uniquely reconstructed from:
$$
(x_i(t), v_i(t)) = \text{Interpolate}\left( \{(x_e, v_e) : e \in \text{Ancestors}(i, t)\} \right)
$$

**B. Swarm Configuration Reconstruction**:
The full swarm configuration $S_t = \{(x_i(t), v_i(t))\}_{i=1}^N$ at any time $t$ can be reconstructed from the set of active episodes at time $t$.

**C. Dynamics Equivalence**:
The enriched Fractal Set is **informationally equivalent** to the full N-particle trajectory history.
:::

**Proof Sketch**:
- Each episode $e$ records the phase space state at a cloning event
- Between cloning events, walker $i$ follows deterministic Langevin dynamics
- Given $(x_e, v_e)$ and knowing Langevin parameters, we can **reconstruct** trajectory forward to next cloning event
- CST structure provides the genealogy ‚Üí complete history

**Status**: ‚úÖ **STRAIGHTFORWARD IF WE ADD VELOCITY AND FITNESS TO EPISODES**

**Implementation Impact**:
- Need to modify `SwarmState` and episode creation in `adaptive_gas.py`
- Storage cost increases: $O(K \cdot d)$ where $K$ = total episodes, $d$ = dimension
- For typical runs: $K \sim 10^4$ episodes, $d = 10$ ‚Üí still manageable

### 2.3. Uniform Constant Preservation

#### **Theorem 2.3.1: N-Uniform Convergence for Discrete QFT**

:::{prf:theorem} Discrete QFT Inherits N-Uniform Bounds
:label: thm-n-uniform-discrete-qft

Assume the enriched Fractal Set preserves full information (Theorem 2.2.2).

Let $\{G_N\}_{N \ge 1}$ be the sequence of graphs generated by Adaptive Gas with $N$ walkers converging to QSD.

Let $C_N$ be the convergence rate for mean-field limit:
$$
W_2(\mu^N_t, \rho_t) \le C_N e^{-\lambda t}
$$

**Then**: If $C_N$ is **N-uniform** (i.e., $\sup_N C_N < \infty$), the discrete field theory on $G_N$ satisfies:

1. **Correlation function bounds**:
   $$
   \left| \langle \phi(e_1) \phi(e_2) \rangle_N - \langle \phi(e_1) \phi(e_2) \rangle_\infty \right| \le \frac{C}{N} e^{-\lambda t}
   $$

2. **Spectral gap**:
   $$
   \lambda_{\text{gap}}(G_N) \ge \lambda_0 > 0 \quad \text{uniformly in } N
   $$

3. **Cluster decomposition**:
   $$
   \langle \phi(e_1) \phi(e_2) \rangle - \langle \phi(e_1) \rangle \langle \phi(e_2) \rangle \le C e^{-m d_g(e_1, e_2)}
   $$
   with mass gap $m > 0$ independent of $N$.
:::

**Proof Strategy**:

**Step 1**: Information preservation (Theorem 2.2.2) implies:
$$
\text{Graph}(G_N) \leftrightarrow \text{Trajectories}(\{(x_i^N(t), v_i^N(t))\}_{i=1}^N)
$$

**Step 2**: Existing mean-field proof gives:
$$
\mu^N_t = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \delta_{(x_i^N(t), v_i^N(t))} \to \rho_t
$$
with N-uniform rate.

**Step 3**: Express discrete field $\phi: \mathcal{V} \to \mathbb{R}$ in terms of particle positions:
$$
\phi(e) = \phi(x_e, v_e) = \text{Field value at episode state}
$$

**Step 4**: Correlation functions are expectation values over swarm:
$$
\langle \phi(e_1) \phi(e_2) \rangle_N = \mathbb{E}_{\mu^N} [\phi(x_1, v_1) \phi(x_2, v_2)]
$$

**Step 5**: Since $\mu^N \to \rho$ with N-uniform rate, correlation functions inherit the bound:
$$
| \mathbb{E}_{\mu^N}[\cdots] - \mathbb{E}_\rho[\cdots] | \le \frac{C}{N}
$$

**Status**: ‚ö†Ô∏è **PLAUSIBLE BUT NEEDS DETAILED PROOF**

**Key Assumption**: Requires that field observables are **Lipschitz continuous** functionals of the measure $\mu$.
